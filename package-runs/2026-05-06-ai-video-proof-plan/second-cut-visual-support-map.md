# Second-Cut Visual Support Map

## Purpose
- Create a narrow edit/pickup checklist for the second cut by identifying the smallest visual-support additions that reduce the screen-only feeling without changing the video promise, inventing proof, or expanding into a redesign.
- Preserve the proof-first boundary: visual support should clarify what is already evidenced or demonstrated, not make the video look more proven than it is.

- The blocker is visual support: the presenter is not seen and the cut is mostly screen capture.

## Evidence boundary
- Real proof:
  - Captured package-run media and documented capture evidence that has been accepted only for the narrow capture-evidence stage.
  - Screen recordings and A-roll/audio files listed in the package-run capture records.
  - The pasted transcript as planning evidence, with its limitations preserved.
- Demonstration:
  - Controlled/reproduced ChatGPT capture used to demonstrate the proof-plan workflow.
  - Proof-plan checklist table or decision table shown as a local planning/demo object.
  - Before/after package revision used to demonstrate the repair process.
- Illustration-only:
  - Any AI-generated B-roll, abstract creator/workflow visuals, synthetic workstation shots, or mood imagery.
  - Such material may support pacing or visual variety only; it must not be presented as evidence of what happened in the package run.
- Unverified / do not imply:
  - Do not imply original ChatGPT UI visual proof exists.
  - Do not imply ChatGPT generated `10 AI Tools Every Creator Should Try in 2026`.
  - Do not imply the prepared weak example is captured AI output.
  - Do not imply final title, final thumbnail, production approval, second-cut readiness, publish readiness, or final edit approval.
  - Do not repeat ChatGPT tool-capability claims as factual claims without separate verification.
