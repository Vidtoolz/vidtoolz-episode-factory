# Operator-Facing Media Creation Plan

Run: `2026-05-06-ai-video-proof-plan`
Working title: `Stop Planning AI Videos Until You Have a Proof Plan`

This plan is for manual production on PRESTO. Hermes describes what media should be created; Mikko controls OBS, Resolve, browser tools, ComfyUI/Kling, camera, audio, and all production software manually.

Current rough-cut problem:
- The rough cut is understandable.
- Pacing and audio are acceptable for this stage.
- Proof/evidence is acceptable for this stage.
- The blocker is visual support: the presenter is not seen and the cut is mostly screen capture.

Production boundary:
- This plan does not approve capture.
- This plan does not mark any clip as approved.
- This plan does not mark second-cut ready.
- This plan does not mark publish ready.
- This plan does not approve final title or thumbnail.
- This plan does not promote project state.

Evidence boundary:
- Pasted transcript = planning evidence.
- Controlled/reproduced ChatGPT capture = demonstration only, not original transcript UI proof.
- `10 AI Tools Every Creator Should Try in 2026` = prepared stress-test example, not ChatGPT output.
- `Stop Adding AI Tools Until They Pass This Workflow Test` = packaging candidate, not final title approval.
- AI-generated visuals, if used, are illustration-only and must not look like evidence.

## Clip cards

### CLIP CARD 01

- Working title: Opening proof question
- Type: A-roll
- Purpose:
  - Solves the biggest current rough-cut weakness: the viewer hears Mikko but does not see him.
  - Gives the opening human authority immediately, so the video feels like a creator/producer judgment piece instead of only a screen-recorded explainer.
  - The viewer needs this because the video is asking them to trust a proof-first production standard; seeing the presenter helps establish that this is an editorial judgment, not just a workflow note.
- Viewer sees:
  - Presenter on camera.
  - Medium close-up or close-up.
  - Stable camera, practical lighting, calm workstation or neutral production background.
  - Background can include desk, monitor glow, camera gear, Resolve/production environment, or a clean wall. Do not rely on any background text being readable.
  - Eye line should be direct to camera.
- I say:
  - A-roll spoken words:
    - "Before you ask AI to write a script, ask one harder question: what will this video prove on screen?"
    - "If the answer is vague, the idea is not ready for production yet."
  - Optional more natural version:
    - "Before I ask AI to help with a script, I want to know one thing: what will this video actually prove on screen?"
    - "If I cannot answer that, I should not be moving into production yet."
- Visual action:
  - Start already composed and looking at camera.
  - Small hand gesture on "prove on screen" is useful but not required.
  - No screen interaction needed.
  - Keep the frame clean; do not add overlays while recording.
- Edit placement:
  - Opening hook / first human-presence insert.
  - Use at or near the first line of the rough cut.
  - Fixes the screen-only feeling in the first 30 seconds.
  - Function: opening hook.
- Capture notes:
  - Duration: 10-15 seconds.
  - Takes: 3 clean takes.
  - Energy: calm, firm, slightly challenging, no hype.
  - Record to camera with clean audio.
  - Usable if the line is clear and Mikko looks present, credible, and relaxed.
- Good enough when:
  - Face is clearly visible.
  - Audio is clean enough to cut into the rough cut.
  - The main phrase "what will this video prove on screen" lands clearly.
  - Minor pauses, a small stumble before/after the usable sentence, or imperfect hand movement are acceptable.
- Redo if:
  - The question is hard to understand.
  - Eye line is badly off.
  - Focus/exposure is distracting.
  - Audio clips, hums badly, or has a loud interruption.
  - The delivery sounds like an ad or motivational speech.
