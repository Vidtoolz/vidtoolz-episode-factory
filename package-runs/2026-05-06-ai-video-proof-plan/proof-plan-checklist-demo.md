## What The Checklist Tests

The proof-plan checklist tests whether an AI-assisted video idea has enough concrete proof to deserve scripting or shooting. It is meant to separate polished planning language from a makeable video with a visible example, demo, comparison, or result.

## Checklist Criteria

| criterion | test question | status for this run |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Viewer promise | What will the viewer be able to do, understand, or decide after watching? | review-needed |
| Proof/demo moment | What specific example, demo, comparison, or result proves the promise on screen? | review-needed |
| Evidence source | Which local file, capture, or inspected artifact supports the proof? | review-needed |
| Production path | Can the proof be captured with available tools, time, and skill? | review-needed |
| Packaging fit | Does the title/thumbnail promise match only what the video can prove? | review-needed |
