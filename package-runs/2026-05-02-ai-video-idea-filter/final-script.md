## Final Script

### Hook

What if the problem is not that AI gives you bad video ideas, but that it gives you too many ideas that sound usable before they have a real viewer promise?

If you are a solo creator, that is dangerous. You can spend a full afternoon scripting an idea that looks impressive in a chat window but turns into a generic video nobody needed. In this video, I am going to use AI as the research assistant, then use a human filter before the script starts.

### Problem / Context

The common mistake is letting AI choose the strategy. A prompt can produce titles, topics, outlines, and thumbnail lines quickly, but output volume is not judgment. When you accept a polished answer without review, you also accept its weak assumptions: vague audience, soft payoff, no proof object, and a title that could belong to any channel.

For VIDTOOLZ, the viewer problem is practical: you need a way to avoid bad AI video ideas before you script, record, or build assets around them.

### Promised Outcome

By the end, you will have a simple filter for AI-generated video ideas so you can keep the research speed without outsourcing taste, positioning, or final judgment.

### Practical Framework

Step 1. Ask AI for options, not decisions. The output is a candidate pool, not a verdict.

Step 2. Score the package, not just the topic. Look at title clarity, thumbnail promise, viewer pain, concrete payoff, proof you can actually show, production difficulty, and the main risk.

Step 3. Force a veto moment. If the idea cannot answer "what will the viewer be able to do differently after watching?", it does not move to script.

Step 4. Keep one rejected example. The rejected idea is useful because it shows what generic looks like and makes the final choice more credible.

### Demonstration / Proof

The recorded demo should show a concrete example on screen: raw AI suggestions on one side, a package scorecard in the middle, and the final human-selected package on the other side.

Example: AI gives a broad idea like "the future of AI video creation." It sounds timely, but the viewer payoff is vague, the proof would become speculative, and the thumbnail promise is weak. That gets rejected.

The stronger package is "Avoid Bad AI Video Ideas Before You Script." It gives the viewer a concrete action, a visible proof object, and a production-feasible demo: compare raw suggestions, apply the filter, and show why one idea survives.

### Decision Criteria

Move the idea forward if it passes these checks:

- It names a specific creator problem.
- It promises a practical outcome the viewer can use.
- It has a thumbnail that visually promises the same thing as the title.
- It can be demonstrated with local screen capture, examples, or artifacts.
- It has clear warning signs and limits, not just enthusiasm.

### Warning Signs

Reject or repair the idea when the title sounds broad, the thumbnail is just a slogan, the proof depends on unsupported claims about AI tools, or the script would mostly explain opinions instead of showing a decision process.

Also reject it when the score looks confident but you cannot find the scoring provenance. A high score can identify a candidate, but it is not production readiness.

### Recap / Takeaway

The takeaway is simple: use AI to widen the option set, then use human judgment to narrow it. AI can help you research faster, but the creator still owns the viewer problem, the payoff, the proof, and the final yes or no.
