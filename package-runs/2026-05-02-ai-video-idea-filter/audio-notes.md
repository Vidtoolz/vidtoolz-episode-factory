# Audio Notes

Planning scope only. These notes define intended audio handling for "Avoid Bad AI Video Ideas Before You Script". They are not captured evidence and do not claim that recording has happened.

## Mic / Capture Notes

- Use the normal VIDTOOLZ mic setup for A-roll and any voiceover pickups.
- Capture a short room-tone sample during the actual recording session so edits can breathe between screen sections.
- Keep screen-recording system audio muted unless a deliberate interface sound is required.
- Do not record unresolved or unapproved sections as final audio.
- Label any future audio files with take number, date, and whether they are A-roll, pickup, or scratch narration.

## Voiceover Notes

- Hook: "What if the problem is not that AI gives you bad video ideas, but that it gives you too many ideas that sound usable before they have a real viewer promise?"
- Main premise: AI can help generate options, but the creator must choose the strategy before scripting.
- Explain the mistake clearly: polished AI output can hide weak assumptions, vague audience, soft payoff, no proof object, and generic packaging.
- Walk through the scorecard criteria in plain language: title clarity, thumbnail promise, viewer pain, concrete payoff, proof object, production difficulty, and main risk.
- Keep the rejected example useful, not mocking: it shows what generic looks like and makes the selected package more credible.
- Emphasize the veto rule: if the idea cannot answer what the viewer can do differently after watching, it does not move to script.
- Treat selected-package score and recommendation as planning artifacts unless repeatable scoring provenance is shown.
- Closing beat: AI is useful as a research assistant, but it should not be the boss of the video strategy.

## Music / SFX Notes

- Keep music and SFX secondary to proof clarity.
- Use simple transition emphasis between raw ideas, scorecard, rejected example, and selected package.
- Avoid dramatic sound design that makes the process feel more certain than the evidence supports.
- Mark final transition points only after screen captures and A-roll are actually reviewed.

## Risks

- Audio must not imply the run is production-approved or ready to shoot before Mikko explicitly approves the Stage 4 plan.
- Any future pickup script should preserve the distinction between planning artifacts, captured evidence, accepted evidence, and production approval.
