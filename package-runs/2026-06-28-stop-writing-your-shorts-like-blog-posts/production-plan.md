## Production Scope

Produce a vertical short built from the approved final script. The point is simple: Shorts should be written as tight spoken and visual sequences, not as mini blog posts.

Target format:

- Vertical short
- 9:16
- 1080x1920
- Presenter-led A-roll with simple visual support

## Approved Visual Approach

- Presenter delivers the approved final script directly and conversationally.
- Visual support contrasts dense blog-style writing with tight spoken Shorts beats.
- Use abstract AI-generated background visuals, kinetic typography, simple contrast cards, and sequence/timeline visuals only where they clarify the script.
- Keep all text readable on mobile.
- Keep pacing tight without adding claims that are not in the final script.

## Presenter A-Roll Requirements

- Record the complete approved final script from final-script.md.
- Delivery should feel spoken, not read from an essay.
- Prioritize clarity, natural rhythm, and fast comprehension.
- Capture room tone or a short silence sample for audio repair.
- Retake unclear hook, realization, rewrite, and final takeaway lines.

## B-Roll / Visual Support Requirements

- Dense blog-style Shorts script page or abstract script document.
- Over-polished script detail visual: too many lines, too many notes, too slow.
- Viewer scrolling past a slow or text-heavy Short.
- Fluff removal visual: long paragraph crossed out and reduced to punchy spoken beats.
- Blog post vs Short contrast card.
- Tight vertical beat sequence or timeline showing spoken beats replacing essay structure.
- Optional thumbnail source frame showing the blog-post vs Short contrast.

## Graphics Requirements

- Opening title or promise card only if it improves the hook.
- `BLOG POST` vs `SHORT` contrast labels.
- Short beat-map or timeline graphic.
- Text-on-screen emphasis for the final rule: Shorts are for listening, not reading.
- Final takeaway card: write like a person, not a post.

## Edit Approach

- Build the hook first.
- Cut out dead air and weak setup.
- Match each visual to a script beat.
- Use typography and contrast cards to clarify the writing difference.
- Keep music, if used, below narration.
- Final watchdown must confirm the title, thumbnail direction, and final edit still match the approved script.
