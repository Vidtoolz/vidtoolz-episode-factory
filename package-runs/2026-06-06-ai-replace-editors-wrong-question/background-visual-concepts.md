## Concept Descriptions

### #1 — Empty Timeline, Bad AI Cuts
- **Visual:** A video editing timeline (Resolve-style), tracks empty except cursor blinking. Then cuts appear — badly timed, mechanical, no rhythm. Cursor jumps erratically.
- **Use:** Hook — "AI just edited a whole video" setup
- **Tone:** Slightly ominous, machine-like

### #2 — Evolution Montage: Old → New
- **Visual:** Rows of editing gear dissolving in sequence: razor blade and tape splicer → Steenbeck flatbed → CRT monitor with Avid interface → modern NLE with timeline. Each phase fades into the next. Warm amber to cool digital.
- **Use:** Setup — "I've seen this movie before"
- **Tone:** Nostalgic, wise

### #3 — Tool Evolution: Accumulation
- **Visual:** Editor silhouette against warm golden light. Tools float around in a circle, appearing one by one: razor blade, tape splicer, Avid keyboard, modern NLE interface, AI prompt icon. They accumulate, don't replace each other.
- **Use:** CTA — "We survived by learning it"
- **Tone:** Hopeful, cinematic

### #4 — Split Screen: AI Cut vs Human Cut
- **Visual:** Vertical split. Left side: cold blue-toned, mechanical cuts appearing on a timeline at mathematically even intervals — sterile, dead. Right side: warm-toned, cuts landing with intentional rhythm — some held, some quick, breathing.
- **Use:** Claim — "editing isn't shrinking, it's expanding"
- **Tone:** Comparative, revealing

### #5 — The Wrong Headline
- **Visual:** Abstract — a newspaper headline or YouTube thumbnail that reads "AI REPLACES EDITORS" floating in dark space. Then it cracks and dissolves, revealing "...IS THE WRONG QUESTION" beneath.
- **Use:** Transition between hook and setup
- **Tone:** Dramatic, reframing

### #6 — Clock / Time-lapse Representing Editing Process
- **Visual:** A clock face, but the hands are edit markers. Time moves forward, cuts appear and disappear, the hands don't race — they're deliberate. Warm light cycles from day to night (late edit sessions).
- **Use:** Example section — "2 a.m. edit suite" moment
- **Tone:** Meditative, dedicated

### #7 — "Adapt or" Text with Tools
- **Visual:** The words "ADAPT OR" glowing in warm light, but the word "DIE" never appears. Instead, editor tools float around the text — keyboard, monitor, timeline, audio meters. The implied threat dissolves into possibility.
- **Use:** Reframe transition
- **Tone:** Defiant, warm

### #8 — Audio Waveform Detail
- **Visual:** Close-up of audio waveform on a studio monitor. Two dialogue tracks visible — the space between them (silence) is highlighted, glowing. Detailed, technical, beautiful.
- **Use:** Example — "two extra seconds of silence"
- **Tone:** Intimate, technical beauty

### #9 — Client Review Room
- **Visual:** A darkened edit suite with a monitor glow. Two chairs in front of the screen — editor and unseen client/director. The human judgment moment. No action, just presence.
- **Use:** Implicit — underscores the human relationship layer
- **Tone:** Quiet, professional

### #10 — Resolve Color Grading Panel
- **Visual:** DaVinci Resolve color wheels interface, warm tones being dialed in. The craft beyond cutting — grading, finishing, the full editor skill set.
- **Use:** Implicit — editing isn't just cutting
- **Tone:** Craft, mastery

### #11 — Editor at Desk, Gears in Reflection
- **Visual:** An editor (from behind or profile) at a workstation in warm light. In the reflection of the monitor: gears turning slowly — not machine gears, almost clockwork — representing cognitive work, judgment, thinking.
- **Use:** Reframe — "you're directing it"
- **Tone:** Focused, intellectual

### #12 — Director's Chair Behind Timeline
- **Visual:** A director's chair (the classic canvas kind) sitting behind a glowing timeline interface. The chair says "EDITOR" on the back. The image says: the editor directs. The machine assists.
- **Use:** Closing beat or final text reveal
- **Tone:** Authoritative, settled
